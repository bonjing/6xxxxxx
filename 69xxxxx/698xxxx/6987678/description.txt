Patricia Lee "Patti" Smith (born December 30, 1946) is an American singer-songwriter,

Called the "Godmother of Punk" her work was a fusion of rock and poetry. Smiths most 
widely known song is "Because the Night", which was co-written with Bruce Springsteen 
and reached number 13 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart in 1978. In 2005, Patti Smith was 
named a Commander of the Ordre des Arts et des Lettres by the French Minister of Culture, 
and in 2007, she was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. On November 17, 2010, 
she won the National Book Award for her memoir Just Kids. She is also a recipient of the 
2011 Polar Music Prize.
