English
The BBC takes you to the North and South Poles, the largest unspoiled natural areas in the world.
This documentary in six parts paints a stunning portrait of a spectacular landscape in terms of size, beauty and extreme environments on Earth with nothing to compare.
Never before have these areas and the animals that live in as much detail mapped.
With a special camera that can withstand terrible temperatures, the team made stunning shots from the air and beneath the surface of the icy ocean.
The journey starts at the Antarctic Convergence, where cold and warm currents collide and where the ocean is rougher than anywhere else in the world.
On the islands within the natural boundary of Antarctica live many rare animals.
In the north we draw about the direction of the Arctic tundra.
This awesome scenery we discover through the eyes of the animals that live there, such as the giant albatross, the Adelie penguins and the polar bear.
While the polar ice melts, Frozen Planet brings a portion of the earth's surface that can forever change soon ...
