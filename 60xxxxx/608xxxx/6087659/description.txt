Inequality: The enemy between us?

[u]The Debate: Is Equality Good for Us?[/u] 

Is society better when we are all equal? Or are hierarchies a natural human reaction that works for us?

[u]Guests:[/u]

[b]Richard Wilkinson[/b] is co-author of [url=http://www.amazon.com/Spirit-Level-Equality-Societies-Stronger/dp/B004A14W0Y/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1294398632&sr=8-1][i]The Spirit Level: Why Greater Equality Makes Societies Stronger[/i][/url] and Professor Emeritus at the University of Nottingham Medical School and honorary professor at University College London. He has played a formative role in international research and his work has been published in 10 languages. He studied economic history at the London School of Economics before training in epidemiology.

[b]William Watson[/b] is the Chair of the Economic Department at McGill University.

[b]Lane Kenworthy[/b] is a sociologist at the University of Arizona where he studies the causes and consequences of poverty, inequality, mobility, employment, economic growth, and social policy in the United States and other affluent countries.

[b]Bob Rae[/b] is the Liberal Foreign Affairs Critic, MP for Toronto Centre, and a Former Premier of Ontario. Visit bobrae.ca.

[b]Sylvia Bashevkin[/b] is Principal of University College, and a Professor in the Department of Political Science, at the University of Toronto. She is the author of Toeing the Lines: Women and Party Politics in English Canada.
