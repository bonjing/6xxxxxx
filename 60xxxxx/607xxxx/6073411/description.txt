This mini series from 1978 was then one of the largest and most ambitious television projects ever, with a budget of $ 25,000,000, 12 feature length episodes, four directors and one hundred speaking roles. The series was based on James Michener panoramic bestseller about the conquest of the Wild West, and particularly about the history of the fictional town of Centennial, Colorado. Dozens of colorful characters, shaped by actors like Robert Conrad, Richard Chamberlain, are reviewed, to pioneers of Indians and trappers, farmers and cowboys. Centennial was one of the first series that also did not shy away serious issues such as racism, the exploitation of Native Americans (a term that has long not been established) and the disrespectful treatment of the land for profit. A classic.
