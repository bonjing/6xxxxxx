                              RIP NOTES                              
                                                                     
                                                                     
           Airing just 10 days before Jim's death, this is           
         the last ever Muppet production under the direction         
         of Jim to air while he was still alive. As we               
         mentioned yesterday, at the time of his death Disney        
         was set to buy the Henson company outright, and in          
         preparation of this a long line of co-productions           
         were happening, including the Here Come The Muppets         
         and Muppets on Location: Days of Swine and Roses            
         stage shows, various walk-arounds, and the still            
         running ever popular Muppet*Vision 3D - the last            
         thing Jim ever directed - which didn't premiere             
         until 1991.                                                 
                                                                     
           The Muppets at Walt Disney World was intended to          
         spread awareness about the merger, by having The            
         Muppets crash WDW, after revealing that Kermit's            
         hometown of Paradise Swamp, Florida is just on the          
         other side of the park. This ends up being an               
         effective cross-promotion of The Muppets and the            
         park as well as Disney properties in general -              
         just as they had intended.
