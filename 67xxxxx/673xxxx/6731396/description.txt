In October 2010, Rovio released a special Halloween edition of game.[71] Angry Birds Halloween, exclusive to Apple iOS at the time and a separate application from the main game, included new levels with Halloween-themed music and graphics.[71] In December 2010, Rovio released Angry Birds Seasons to iOS, Android and Symbian^3 devices.[72][73] Seasons introduced 25 Christmas-themed levels, one for each day leading to the holiday, similar to an Advent calendar.[72][73] All versions include the previously-exclusive Halloween levels and are offered as separate, stand-alone paid applications, with the exception of the free, ad-supported Android version;[74] Angry Birds Halloween users on iOS received the Seasons levels as a free upgrade.[72] The Halloween version was given the episode title "Trick or Treat", while the Christmas episode was entitled "Season's Greedings". In February 2011, Rovio released a new Valentine's Day update to Angry Birds Seasons, entitled "Hogs and Kisses", complete with new themed levels and graphics, as well as the option to send Angry Birds-themed Valentine's Day messages through Facebook.[75] In March 2011, Rovio released a new St. Patrick's Day update, entitled "Go Green, Get Lucky",[76] followed by an Easter update, entitled "Easter Eggs", in April 2011[77] and a summer update, "Summer Pignic", in June 2011.[78] In September 2011, "Mooncake Festival" was released in conjunction with the Chinese Mid-Autumn Festival.[79]
