The Remix is a remix album by American recording artist Lady Gaga. Released in Japan on March 3, 2010,
it contains remixes of the songs from her first studio album, The Fame (2008), and her third extended
play, The Fame Monster (2009). A revised version of the tracklist was prepared for release in additional
markets, beginning with Mexico on May 3, 2010. A number of recording artists have produced the songs,
including Pet Shop Boys, Passion Pit and The Sound of Arrows. The remixed versions feature both uptempo
and downtempo compositions, with altered vocals from Gaga.


The album received mixed reviews from contemporary critics, but some noted how Gaga was able to sell
the songs from The Fame in new and novel ways. It reached the top of charts in Greece and the Dance
Electronic Albums chart of Billboard magazine in the United States. In other nations, The Remix charted
within the top ten in Belgium (Flanders and Wallonia region), Canada, Ireland, Japan, Mexico,
the United Kingdom and the Billboard 200 chart of United States, while reaching the top-twenty in others.
It was certified platinum in Japan and received gold certification in Belgium, Brazil and Russia.
Worldwide the album has sold over 500,000 copies, and it is among the best-selling remix albums of all time.
