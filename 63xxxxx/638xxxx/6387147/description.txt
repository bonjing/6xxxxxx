[u]The Debate: Still Lamenting for a Nation?[/u] 

Forty-five years after George Grant's [i]Lament for a Nation[/i], what is the state of Canadian nationalism?

[u]Guests[/u]

[b]Michael Byers[/b] is a professor in the Department of Political Science at the University of British Columbia, where he holds the Canada research chair in global politics and international law.

[b]John Duffy[/b] is a principal at StrategyCorp, and a Liberal advisor.

[b]Andrew Potter[/b] is a public affairs columnist at Maclean's, and author of The Authenticity Hoax: How We Get Lost Finding Ourselves.

[b]David Warren[/b] is a columnist with the Ottawa Citizen.

[b]Mel Watkins[/b] is professor emeritus of political science and economics at the University of Toronto.
