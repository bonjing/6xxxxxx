Loud is the fifth studio album by Barbadian recording artist Rihanna, released November 12, 2010 on Def Jam Recordings. Recording sessions and production for the album took place during February to August 2010, partially during Rihanna's Last Girl on Earth Tour. Rihanna worked with several record producers, including StarGate, The Runners, Polow da Don, Tricky Stewart, and Alex da Kid, among others. Loud heavily incorporates dance-pop and pop to offer up-tempo, sing-along and club-oriented songs. Loud also marks Rihanna's first return to a dancehall-influenced sound since 2006's A Girl Like Me.
The album debuted at number one on the Canadian and Swiss Albums Chart. Loud entered at number three on the US Billboard 200 chart, selling 207,000 copies in its first week in the United States. It produced four singles, including the international hits "Only Girl (In the World)", "What's My Name?", and "S&M". Upon its release, Loud received positive reviews from most music critics, who complimented its upbeat material and Rihanna's performance. It also earned Rihanna a Grammy Award for Best Singles
The album's lead single "Only Girl (In the World)" was released on September 10, 2010.[32] The song was sent to US mainstream and rhythmic radio on September 21, 2010.[33][34] The song has currently peaked at number one on the US Billboard Hot 100. The song has also reached number one in the UK, Canada, Australia, Austria, Belgium (Wallonia), Ireland, Israel, Italy, New Zealand, Norway, and Slovakia.[35][36] The song won a Grammy Award in the Best Dance Recording category at 53rd Grammy Awards.[37] "What's My Name?" was released as the second single of the album on October 29, 2010 and the video has already been shot.[38] On October 21, 2010, Billboard reported that the version of the single featuring Drake had made its hot shot debut on the Hot R&B/Hip Hop Songs chart at number sixty-seven.[39] It was released in some European countries on October 29, 2010 and later in the US on November 1.[40] It reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100, giving Rihanna her ninth number-one hit on the chart.[41][42]
"Raining Men" was initially released to urban radio on December 7, 2010 and peaked on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs Chart at number fifty-seven.[17] "S&M" was released to mainstream radio on January 25, 2011.[43] Rihanna had tipped the song to be a single early on, with plans for an "unexpected" music video.[20][38] "S&M" charted successfully peaking within the top ten in twelve countries, including number one in Australia, number two on the US Billboard Hot 100 and New Zealand and number three on the Canadian Singles Chart and the UK Singles Chart.[44][45][46] "Raining Men" was also re-released to urban radio on January 25, 2011.[43] At the beginning of March 2011, Rihanna asked fans to help her select her next single. Via twitter, fans were asked to choose from "Cheers (Drink to That)", "Man Down", "California King Bed" or "Fading". The most popular choice will have its video filmed at the end of March 2011.[47] On March 12, 2011 it was confirmed that fans had selected "California King Bed" as the next single from the album.[48] The non-single track "Man Down" debuted on the Billboard R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart at 84 in the week of April 9, 2011.
