SYNOPSIS

Women of Tibet: Gyalyum Chemo - The Great Mother is the first of a trilogy of 
films about the women of Tibet and recounts the life story of Dekyi Tsering, 
the mother of one of the world's leading ambassadors for peace, His Holiness 
the 14th Dalai Lama.

During the course of her life, Tsering, known by Tibetans as "Gyalyum Chemo" 
or "Great Mother," gave birth to 16 children, three of whom were recognized 
as incarnate lamas, or tulkus. This story is uniquely Tibetan, yet it shares 
the same core qualities of all universal Great Mother stories: loss and resurrection, 
love and sacrifice, and the courage to survive.

Dr. Marion Woodman, Alice Walker and Angeles Arrien speak about the often invisible 
yet ever-powerful impact of a mother's love, revealing the importance of the 
Great Mother archetype and its relevance today.
