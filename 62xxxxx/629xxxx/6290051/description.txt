The UEFA Champions League is an annual association football cup competition organised by UEFA since 1955 for the top football clubs in Europe. It is widely considered to be the most prestigious football club competition in the world.


The UEFA Champions League Anthem was written by English composer Tony Britten.

In 1992, UEFA commissioned Britten to arrange an anthem for the UEFA Champions League which commenced in August 1992, and he adapted George Frideric Handel's "Zadok the Priest" from the Coronation Anthems. The chorus in the three official languages used by UEFA: English, German, and French.
The anthem's chorus is played before each UEFA Champions League game, as well as at the beginning and end of television broadcasts of the matches.
