William L. Calhoun Jr. (born on August 9, 1970), better known by his stage name WC (pronounced Dub-C), is an American rapper. He was born in Texas, but moved to South Central Los Angeles at an early age. He originally was a rapper in the group Low Profile, and later formed his group WC and the Maad Circle. WC and the Maad Circle also included fellow rapper Coolio, producer Sir Jinx and DJ Crazy Toones. He released two albums with his group, Ain't a Damn Thang Changed in 1991 and Curb Servin' in 1995. He later started a solo career and has released three solo albums, The Shadiest One in 1998, Ghetto Heisman in 2002, and Guilty by Affiliation in 2007. He is also well known for being a member of the rap supergroup Westside Connection with fellow West Coast rappers Ice Cube and Mack 10, He released two albums with this group, Bow Down in 1996 and Terrorist Threats in 2003.
