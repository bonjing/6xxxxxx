The Siegfried Line

The Siegfried Line (or West Wall) between 1936 and 1945, commissioned by Adolf Hitler built as a reaction to the French Maginot Line, a huge fortress system along the German border.
The operation of less than 630 (!) kilometers Siegfried is psychologically and strategically designed
The strategic advantage is already evident in 1939. Only 100,000 to 200,000 German soldiers in the Siegfried line stand against 100 French divisions (800,000 men!).
In May 1940 the Germans abandoned their line through the Ardennes to invade France.
In 1944, the old line a safe stop for the retreating Germans.
The awe of allied side for the Siegfried Line Hitler Germany gets a chance to reorganize. A fierce and bitter struggle of 6 months follows.
