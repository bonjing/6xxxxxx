[u]The Debate: The Meaning of Man[/u]

From head of the household to the butt of jokes, men have gone from the top of the world to underachieving drop outs. Is it finally time to feel sorry for the man?  

[u]Guests:[/u]

[b]Mark Regnerus[/b] is an associate professor of sociology at the University of Texas at Austin and the author of [i]Premarital Sex in America: How Young Americans Meet, Mate and Think about Marrying[/i].

[b]Micah Toub[/b] is a Globe and Mail relationships columnist.

[b]Jordan Peterson[/b] is a clinical psychologist, a professor of psychology at the University of Toronto, and author of [i]Maps of Meaning: The Architecture of Belief[/i].

[b]John Intini[/b] is senior editor at Maclean's.
