In the mid 60s he founded together with his fellow students Nick Mason and Richard Wright a combo that changed the name quite a few times before they found the right name with "Pink Floyd". Their debut "Piper At The Gates Of Dawn" (1967) lays the foundations for a musical legend. One year later after the suck of the charismatic singer and guitarist Syd Barrett, the bass player Waters took over and created with Pink Floyd one of the most influential bands of the 70s. His existential and often oppressive lyrics lead to milestones such as "The Dark Side Of The Moon" (1973) and "The Wall" (1980). In this time Pink Floyd reinvent the stadium rock new.
